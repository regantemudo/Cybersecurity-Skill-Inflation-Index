The Cyber Security GRC Analyst will support the organisation’s cyber security governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) activities, with a focus on cyber security metrics and reporting, policy and standards management, and security compliance aligned to recognised frameworks.

The role works closely with cyber security and technology teams to ensure controls are documented, measured, and reported in line with ISO/IEC 27001 and other relevant frameworks adopted by the organisation.

What You Will Do

Cyber Security Metrics & Reporting

    Collect, analyse, and report cyber security metrics.
    Identify trends and support discussions on areas of underperformance.
    Prepare clear reports and presentations for governance and leadership forums.

Policy, Standards & Documentation

    Support the review, update, and maintenance of cyber security policies, standards, and procedures.
    Perform documentation quality checks to ensure accuracy, consistency, and alignment with framework requirements.
    Maintain structured and auditable documentation repositories.

Compliance, Frameworks & Audit Support

    Support ISO/IEC 27001 implementation and ongoing compliance.
    Assist with compliance activities aligned to other frameworks used by the organisation (e.g. NIST CSF).
    Participate in internal and external audits as required.
    Track audit findings and corrective actions to closure.

Stakeholder Engagement & Enablement

    Engage with stakeholders to gather inputs and explain compliance and reporting requirements.
    Act as a point of contact for routine cyber governance and compliance queries.

Experience

 What You Will Have 

    3–5 years’ experience in a Cyber Security GRC, Information Security, Risk, or Compliance role.
    Experience supporting ISO/IEC 27001 implementation, audits, or BAU compliance.
    Exposure to additional frameworks such as NIST CSF or SOC 2 is desirable.

Skills

    Strong analytical capability and experience working with metrics and reporting.
    Strong documentation and quality-assurance skills.
    Good understanding of cyber security governance, risk, and compliance concepts.
    Proficiency with reporting, documentation, and presentation tools.
    Clear and confident communication skills.
